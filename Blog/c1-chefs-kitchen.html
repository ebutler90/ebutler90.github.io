Covering: Thoughts on DBC Experience

Monday, December 29, 2014

Based on the video, what's your take on the DBC experience? What is your impression of DBC? How do you see yourself engaging with this type of culture?
Based on the video, I was able to draw a few conclusions on what the DBC expereince will be like. I believe that this expereince will first and foremost be what I make of it. Shereef's comments about understanding that this is all an "experiment" and that everyone should walk in with the creative, determined mindset really spoke to me. I truly feel that being a part of DBC is a privilege, and all participants should understand that and make the most of this experience. I'm a firm believer in creating the life that you want to live, and I am under the impression that DBC will allow you to do just that. I am so used to being pushed cookie-cutter curriculums and learning situations, that I'm excited to be able to engage in this type of learning-culture. I am admittedly a bit nervous. I don't have a huge amount of experience with always working in pair-situations, so this will be a very new thing for me. I'm excited to engage with my cohort-mates though, and meld into this culture of sharing and mutual growth.

Have your expectations of DBC changed? If so, how?
My expectations of DBC have not really changed. I was introduced to the program through a friend, and had the pleasure of visiting the NY campus twice, so I was aware of the culture, and knew the kind of environment I was getting myself into.

Are you excited to participate in this kind of learning environment? Does it make you nervous?

I'm very very excited to participate in DBC's learning environment. But I'm also so nervous. I want to be great at this, and I know that I have it in me to be amazing. But those pesky nerves are still fighting their way into my psyche. I think that things will be a bit better once I actually get to the in-person immersive stage.
